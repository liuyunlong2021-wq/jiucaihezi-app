# Master Shot Checklist

Use this checklist when building `master_shot_environment_manifest`, `scene_zone_map`, and `master_shot_lighting_design`.

The goal is not to force every scene to include every item.

The goal is to prevent omissions in the one reusable master-shot truth.

## 1. Camera truth check

Confirm that the chosen master shot clearly locks:

- the decisive axis side
- the main confrontation or action corridor
- the hero prop play area if one exists
- the main entrance / exit relation
- the room scale

Reject the shot if it is:

- too symmetric and generic
- too wide to feel like the scripted room
- missing the path where key action must happen
- impossible to reuse for later inserts or blocking

## 2. Style inheritance check

Translate `StyleDesign` into visible scene behavior:

- where the key light comes from
- whether the source is window light, practical light, overhead light, mixed light, or outdoor ambient spill
- whether the fill is weak or even
- whether contrast should stay soft, medium, or high
- whether the room should feel cool, warm, mixed, or neutral
- whether practical fixtures are visible but inactive, visible and active, or absent

Do not stop at adjectives.

Always explain how the selected style appears in this exact room.

## 3. Zone map check

Name the visible play areas in reusable production language.

Common examples:

- `door_zone`
- `entry_zone`
- `walkway_zone`
- `bed_zone`
- `sofa_zone`
- `table_zone`
- `desk_window_zone`
- `storage_zone`
- `report_drop_zone`
- `weapon_pickup_zone`

Choose only the zones that are actually useful later.

## 4. Environment manifest checklist

Check visible environment by layer:

### Foreground

- bed edge
- table edge
- sofa arm
- foreground chair
- rug or floor break
- practical small objects

### Midground

- main play area
- prop landing area
- corridor or walkway
- seating position
- dominant furniture

### Background

- door
- window
- curtains
- desk
- shelves
- wardrobe
- cabinets
- wall treatment

### Vertical surfaces

- wall color and finish
- switches
- art
- stains, marks, cracks, wear
- door frame
- window frame

### Floor and ceiling

- floor material
- floor color
- visible floor objects
- ceiling type
- ceiling fixture

## 5. Empty plate rule

Default master-shot scene asset should be:

- empty environment plate
- no hero person
- no silhouette
- no partial arm or shoulder
- no baked-in action

Only allow background crowd when the environment truly requires it.

## 6. Common failure modes

Avoid these:

- hotel-room polish when the script needs a cramped rental
- luxury scale when the script needs an ordinary room
- window light from the wrong side
- blocked corridor where later action needs a clean path
- too many decorative objects that fight the story
- not enough lived-in detail, making the room feel fake
- multiple contradictory focal areas
- broken perspective or distorted architecture
